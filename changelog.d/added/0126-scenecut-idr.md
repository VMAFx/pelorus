- **`vf_pelorus_scenecut`** (`ffmpeg-patches/files/vf_pelorus_scenecut.c`): scene-cut → **forced IDR**, a vendor-neutral encoder-steering consumer that completes the ADR-0114 strategy on the temporal/structural axis. A **metadata-only** filter (`AVFILTER_FLAG_METADATA_ONLY` — no shader, no GPU work, no pixels touched) that reads `PEL_SEC_MOTION.has_scene_cut` (the flag `vf_pelorus_mc_vulkan` already emits, ADR-0116) from the frame's Pelorus side data via `pel_blob_find_section` and, on a cut frame, sets `frame->pict_type = AV_PICTURE_TYPE_I` (+ `AV_FRAME_FLAG_KEY`) so the downstream encoder opens a fresh GOP exactly at the cut instead of predicting across the unrelated picture and re-keying at the next periodic IDR. Codec-agnostic — `pict_type == I` is honoured as a forced keyframe by x264/x265/NVENC/QSV/SVT-AV1 with **no per-encoder patch** (unlike the ROI/delta-QP tiers); links `libpelorus` but is **not** gated on Vulkan. AVOption: `force_idr` (bool, default on; `0` = transparent pass-through). Recommended placement: after `hwdownload`, just before the encoder (the motion side data rides the frame via `av_frame_copy_props`). No interop ABI bump — it *consumes* the pre-reserved `PEL_SEC_MOTION` section. Producer + consumer + mechanism are build-verified; the end-to-end BD-rate proof (an A/B of scene-cut-aligned GOPs vs periodic IDR on real multi-shot content, ADR-0111 methodology) is a documented follow-up — no number claimed yet. (ADR-0126)
