- **`vf_pelorus_deblock_vulkan`** (`ffmpeg-patches/files/vf_pelorus_deblock_vulkan.c`): re-encode **deblock / dering** — a single-pass, zero-copy Vulkan compute (`FF_VK_REP_FLOAT` UNORM, bit-depth-agnostic, luma only; chroma passes through) that smooths the prior codec's DCT block-edge discontinuities ("blocking") so the new encoder does not waste bits coding them as false residual. The largest real pre-encode corpus is re-transcode of already-compressed sources, which carry a step at the prior transform grid's boundaries. At that grid (`bsize`), for each pixel within `edge` of a boundary the filter applies a weak `[1 2 1]` low-pass *across* the boundary, **gated by the cross-boundary step**: a small step (below `thr`) is a block-edge artefact and is smoothed; a large step (above `thr`) is real structure and is preserved untouched. AVOptions: `bsize` (8), `edge` (1), `thr` (0.06), `str` (0.6), `planes` (0x1 = luma). A pure pixel transform — it does not link libpelorus, emits no interop side data (deps-only registration), and complements the deband stage (block edges vs. flats). Reference shader `libpelorus/shaders/pelorus_deblock.comp` kept in lockstep. Build-verified and glslang-clean; the on-content tuning of the defaults and the SSIMULACRA2 / BD-rate proof on a real re-transcode corpus are documented follow-ups (no quality number claimed yet). Shipped as patch 0016. (ADR-0127)
