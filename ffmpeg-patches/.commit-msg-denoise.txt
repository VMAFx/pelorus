feat(denoise): add vf_pelorus_denoise_vulkan (temporal denoise)

An edge-preserving spatio-temporal denoise filter — the biggest BD-rate
pre-encode lever. A single Vulkan compute pass over a causal window (the
current frame + N previous frames held in VRAM, no motion compensation):
an NLM-lite joint bilateral whose range weight is a 3x3-patch SSD (a real
edge has high patch SSD across it, so it refuses to average across the
edge) blended with per-pixel-gated temporal averaging (a delta above tcut
breaks the walk so motion / scene-cuts cannot ghost), then a dry/wet mix.

The exec is the hand-rolled explicit path (modeled on vf_pelorus_analyze):
with meta=1 it free-rides a residual reduction on the denoise dispatch and
emits the pre-reserved PEL_SEC_DENOISE interop section (residual energy,
applied strength, noise-sigma estimate, PSNR-vs-input); with meta=0 the
SSBO / readback / sync are skipped. The standalone reference shader
libpelorus/shaders/pelorus_denoise.comp and the inline GLSL are kept in
lockstep.

Codec-agnostic: the denoise is on the source, independent of the encoder.

AVOptions: sigma, sigmac, sigmat, strength, strengthc, blend, tdecay, tcut,
patch, prev, protect, planes, meta, mc (motion-compensated temporal taps,
ADR-0131), tile (opt-in shared-memory tiling of the spatial window, ADR-0134;
bit-identical, a throughput win on bandwidth-limited GPUs). Host-side param
contract in libpelorus/include/pelorus/denoise.h.

Requires libpelorus >= 0.1.0 (pkg-config) when the filter is enabled.

ADR: docs/adr/0112-temporal-denoise.md
