feat(nvenc): feed the Pelorus MV field to NVENC external ME hints

Stock nvenc.c always runs the ASIC's own motion search and cannot be seeded
with externally-computed motion vectors. This adds a `pelorus_me_hints`
AVOption (default off, H.264/HEVC) that, when set, reads the per-block
integer-pel MV field a vf_pelorus_mc_vulkan stage emits as the PEL_SEC_MOTION
interop section (AV_FRAME_DATA_SEI_UNREGISTERED, UUID-keyed) and translates it
into NVENC's external-ME-hint input: enableExternalMEHints +
maxMEHintCountsPerBlock at init, NV_ENC_PIC_PARAMS.meExternalHints +
meHintCountsPerBlock per frame (under a NVENC_HAVE_EXTERNAL_ME_HINTS guard,
SDK 8.1+). One L0 candidate per 16x16 block; the producer's bsize grid is
resampled onto the 16x16 hint grid by center-block sampling. mvx/mvy are
clamped to the NVENC bitfield ranges (S12 / S10).

Value is ENCODE SPEED, not quality: the ASIC seeds/short-circuits its own
search from our hints. No quality or speedup number ships here — the MV field
is wired into NVENC's external-ME input; a measured on-HW encode-speed A/B is
the documented follow-up (ADR-0114 Tier 3 / ADR-0116).

Generality: default off (zero behaviour change); the external-ME-hint
capability is runtime-probed (NV_ENC_CAPS_SUPPORT_MEONLY_MODE) with graceful
degrade + a one-shot warning; AV1 is skipped (it uses the different
NVENC_EXTERNAL_ME_SB_HINT struct) rather than fed the wrong layout; the 16x16
block geometry is the SDK-documented external-hint granularity for AVC/HEVC,
not any one GPU's coding-tree size. The Pelorus blob is parsed inline (no
libavcodec->libpelorus link for a default-off feature) against the frozen
append-only interop ABI (R1..R6).

If a frame carries no PEL_SEC_MOTION section the hint buffer is left unset for
that frame, so NVENC runs its own search and behaviour is unchanged.

ADR: docs/adr/0116-pelorus-mc.md (consumer section), docs/adr/0114-encoder-steering.md (Tier 3)
